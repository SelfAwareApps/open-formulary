About Mobile Formulary

Mobile Formulary was built as an exploratory research project at the NHS Hackday to explore better ways of providing health professionals with access to drug dosage information via their mobile phones whilst they are working on wards.

It was built by a group of doctors and developers who gave up their weekend and to try and make the NHS run a bit smoother.

Since this is a research project it should not be used in real medical situations, BNF.org remains the definitive source, and the copyright of the data remains theirs.

The BNF is an an amazing resource, the result of an editorial team for collating, writing and continually reviewing the data.

Want to get involved?

The code for this project is available to fork on Github. You can find out more by joining the email discussion group here or by following @nhshackday on Twitter. We would love feedback from anyone in the medical profession on how best we can collaborate to improve the app.

Will this become a fully working app?

We will be talking to the Royal Pharmaceutical Society of Great Britain to see how we can best collaborate and take forward the things we have learnt.
